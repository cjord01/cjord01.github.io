Week #5: Cultural

Peer Pairing & Feedback

For the most part I've really enjoyed the pairing experience. Initially I was surprised to hear that pairing was a common practice in coding. Now that I've had some experience with it I can see how its a more productive approach. There are times when I'm working along, hit something I don't understand, and I'll be stuck there for hours. When pairing, my partner will often save the day and we're able to blast through the obstacle and keep moving forward.

I've been somewhat surprised at how strong the communication has been in most pairing sessions. Programmers aren't known for their amazing interpersonal skills. We're breaking stereotypes here guys. I've found that most partners have been easy to get along with, patient, and knowledgeable. Most have been willing and able to help me better understand concepts that I'm not completely clear on. This has allowed me to get a lot more out of pairing sessions than when I work independently.

The most useful piece of feedback that I've received is that I withdrew when I didn't seem to understand the code. I would agree that this is true. In many of the pairing sessions I've been a little intimidated by my partner's coding knowledge. I often feel like others have a better understanding of the material. There have been times when I've felt so out of my league that my instinct was to just get out of the way. This is not the right approach. I should be better utilizing these opportunities to ask questions and see if my partner can breakdown the code for me. This is something I will be more aware of moving forward.
